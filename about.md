    <p>I&rsquo;d been building quietly on the side for over a year: a one-person business running on an operating system I designed from scratch. 17 production services across 7 domains. Workflows that run with and without AI inference. Secure data pipelines and closed improvement loops. Compliance enforcement as a service that workflows call, not a checklist someone runs. A real business that not only runs, but continuously improves, autonomously.</p>

    <p>Which led me to building another system: one for working with and governing AI itself. But, AI capabilities keep changing, fast. Models forget everything between sessions. Outputs drift. A system that worked last month breaks when the model updates. What if I lost control? I had to solve these problems to reach my vision of an autonomous business.</p>

    <p>Structured context so every session starts with full operational awareness. Task scaffolding that breaks work into typed, verifiable units with defined inputs, outputs, and acceptance criteria before execution starts. Verification loops that catch drift before it reaches production. Strict permission tiers so the AI knows exactly what it can do autonomously, what requires approval, and what is permanently off-limits. Audit trails, sync and async coordination channels, hooks that enforce rules at the boundary.</p>

    <p>That system is what I now bring to clients. The same discipline: governance before execution, structure before scale. Applied to businesses that are adopting AI and hitting the walls that come with it.</p>

    <h2 class="no-border">How I work</h2>
    <p>Every engagement runs on the same correction loop I run on my own production systems. When something breaks &mdash; and it does &mdash; we trace it to the decision that produced it, fix the decision, and the symptom takes care of itself. No paper-over. No firefighting. The four stories on the home page are how that pattern actually plays out in real production work.</p>
    <p>I work with one client at a time on builds. Two simultaneously on retainers, max. The cap is operational &mdash; at the depth of work I do, more than that drops quality.</p>

# AI Code/Tool Governance Audit

## Introduction

As AI tools become embedded in engineering workflows, the question of governance is no longer theoretical. Who decides which tools get used? What happens when a tool fails, leaks data, or produces incorrect output?

## The Core Challenge

Most teams adopt AI tools reactively. There is no policy, no audit trail, no incident response plan. This works until it does not.

A governance audit forces the hard questions: What AI tools are currently in use? Are they approved? Do they have access to production data? What is the blast radius if one is compromised?

## Key Areas to Audit

### 1. Tool Inventory
Start with a full inventory. You likely have more AI tooling than you think. Each is a potential attack surface.

### 2. Data Access Review
For each tool: what data can it access? Many AI integrations are granted broad permissions during setup and never reviewed.

### 3. Incident Response
If an AI tool produces harmful output or is compromised, what is the response plan? Most organizations have none.

## Conclusion

AI governance is not about slowing down adoption. It is about making adoption sustainable.
